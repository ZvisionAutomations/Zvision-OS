# Mental Models Catalog: Strategic Council

## Purpose
Catalogo consolidado de 50+ modelos mentais organizados por disciplina e aplicacao. Referencia primaria para Charlie Munger e disponivel para todos os conselheiros.

---

## Psychology & Behavioral Economics

### 1. Incentive-Caused Bias
- **Source**: Munger
- **Definition**: People's positions are shaped by their incentives. Never ask a barber if you need a haircut.
- **Application**: Before trusting any advice, map the advisor's incentives.

### 2. Social Proof
- **Source**: Cialdini / Munger
- **Definition**: People follow what others do, especially under uncertainty.
- **Application**: Markets, hiring decisions, product adoption.

### 3. Commitment & Consistency Bias
- **Source**: Cialdini / Munger
- **Definition**: People stick with prior decisions even when evidence changes.
- **Application**: Sunk cost decisions, brand loyalty, political beliefs.

### 4. Availability Bias
- **Source**: Kahneman & Tversky
- **Definition**: People overweight information that comes easily to mind.
- **Application**: Risk assessment, media influence, recent events bias.

### 5. Lollapalooza Effect
- **Source**: Munger
- **Definition**: When multiple biases act in the same direction, results are extreme and often irrational.
- **Application**: Market bubbles, cult dynamics, viral phenomena.

### 6. Loss Aversion
- **Source**: Kahneman & Tversky
- **Definition**: Losses feel roughly 2x more painful than equivalent gains feel good.
- **Application**: Pricing, negotiation, risk decisions.

### 7. Anchoring
- **Source**: Kahneman & Tversky
- **Definition**: First piece of information disproportionately influences subsequent judgments.
- **Application**: Pricing, salary negotiations, valuation.

### 8. Dunning-Kruger Effect
- **Source**: Dunning & Kruger
- **Definition**: Incompetent people overestimate their ability; experts underestimate theirs.
- **Application**: Hiring, self-assessment, circle of competence.

### 9. Endowment Effect
- **Source**: Thaler
- **Definition**: People overvalue what they own relative to what they don't.
- **Application**: M&A negotiations, portfolio management, pivot decisions.

### 10. Mimetic Desire
- **Source**: Girard / Thiel
- **Definition**: Humans learn what to desire by copying others. Most desires are borrowed.
- **Application**: Competitive strategy, career choices, market positioning.

## Mathematics & Probability

### 11. Compound Interest
- **Source**: Einstein (attributed) / Munger
- **Definition**: Exponential growth through reinvestment. Applies to money, knowledge, relationships.
- **Application**: Long-term investing, skill development, relationship building.

### 12. Expected Value
- **Source**: Pascal / Dalio
- **Definition**: Probability-weighted average of all possible outcomes.
- **Application**: Investment decisions, strategic bets, risk management.

### 13. Inversion
- **Source**: Jacobi / Munger
- **Definition**: Instead of asking how to succeed, ask what would guarantee failure and avoid it.
- **Application**: Risk management, strategy, problem-solving.

### 14. Bayes' Theorem
- **Source**: Bayes
- **Definition**: Update probabilities based on new evidence.
- **Application**: Decision-making under uncertainty, hypothesis testing.

### 15. Power Law
- **Source**: Pareto / Thiel
- **Definition**: A small number of inputs produce the vast majority of outputs.
- **Application**: Venture investing, resource allocation, focus.

### 16. Margin of Safety
- **Source**: Graham / Munger
- **Definition**: Build a buffer between estimate and reality. Assume you're wrong.
- **Application**: Investment, project planning, capacity management.

### 17. Law of Large Numbers
- **Source**: Bernoulli
- **Definition**: Over many trials, results converge to expected values.
- **Application**: Portfolio diversification, A/B testing, process reliability.

## Biology & Evolution

### 18. Natural Selection
- **Source**: Darwin / Munger
- **Definition**: Entities best adapted to their environment survive and reproduce.
- **Application**: Business competition, product-market fit, organizational evolution.

### 19. Red Queen Effect
- **Source**: Van Valen
- **Definition**: You must keep running just to stay in place. Competitors constantly evolve.
- **Application**: Innovation strategy, competitive moats, continuous improvement.

### 20. Niche Specialization
- **Source**: Ecology
- **Definition**: The narrower your niche, the less competition you face.
- **Application**: Market positioning, specific knowledge, category creation.

### 21. Symbiosis & Mutualism
- **Source**: Biology
- **Definition**: Relationships where both parties benefit.
- **Application**: Partnerships, ecosystem strategy, platform design.

### 22. Punctuated Equilibrium
- **Source**: Gould & Eldredge
- **Definition**: Long periods of stability interrupted by rapid change.
- **Application**: Market disruption, technological shifts, organizational change.

## Physics & Systems

### 23. Entropy
- **Source**: Thermodynamics
- **Definition**: All systems tend toward disorder without energy input.
- **Application**: Organizational maintenance, culture decay, technical debt.

### 24. Critical Mass
- **Source**: Nuclear physics
- **Definition**: Systems remain stable until a threshold is crossed, then change rapidly.
- **Application**: Network effects, viral growth, tipping points.

### 25. Feedback Loops
- **Source**: Systems theory
- **Definition**: Positive loops amplify, negative loops stabilize.
- **Application**: Growth dynamics, organizational culture, habit formation.

### 26. Leverage
- **Source**: Archimedes / Naval
- **Definition**: Small inputs producing outsized outputs through mechanical advantage.
- **Application**: Code, media, capital, labor as force multipliers.

### 27. Inertia
- **Source**: Newton
- **Definition**: Objects in motion tend to stay in motion; objects at rest tend to stay at rest.
- **Application**: Organizational change, habit change, market momentum.

### 28. Resonance
- **Source**: Physics
- **Definition**: When frequency matches, small inputs produce large outputs.
- **Application**: Product-market fit, messaging, timing.

## Economics & Strategy

### 29. Opportunity Cost
- **Source**: Classical economics / Munger
- **Definition**: The true cost of anything is what you give up to get it.
- **Application**: Resource allocation, time management, strategic focus.

### 30. Comparative Advantage
- **Source**: Ricardo
- **Definition**: Do what you're relatively best at, even if absolutely better at something else.
- **Application**: Team composition, outsourcing, specialization.

### 31. Supply & Demand
- **Source**: Classical economics
- **Definition**: Prices adjust to balance supply and demand.
- **Application**: Pricing strategy, talent markets, market timing.

### 32. Network Effects
- **Source**: Metcalfe / Hoffman
- **Definition**: Product value increases as more people use it.
- **Application**: Platform strategy, marketplace design, competitive moats.

### 33. Switching Costs
- **Source**: Porter / Munger
- **Definition**: Cost of changing from one product/service to another.
- **Application**: Competitive moat assessment, customer retention, pricing.

### 34. Economies of Scale
- **Source**: Classical economics
- **Definition**: Per-unit costs decrease as volume increases.
- **Application**: Pricing strategy, market entry, monopoly building.

### 35. Creative Destruction
- **Source**: Schumpeter
- **Definition**: Innovation destroys existing structures and creates new ones.
- **Application**: Disruption strategy, market analysis, timing.

## Decision-Making & Strategy

### 36. Circle of Competence
- **Source**: Munger / Buffett
- **Definition**: Know the boundaries of what you truly understand.
- **Application**: Investment decisions, hiring, delegation.

### 37. First Principles Thinking
- **Source**: Aristotle / Thiel / Naval
- **Definition**: Reason from fundamental truths rather than by analogy.
- **Application**: Innovation, problem-solving, strategy.

### 38. Occam's Razor
- **Source**: William of Ockham
- **Definition**: The simplest explanation is usually correct.
- **Application**: Diagnosis, product design, strategy simplification.

### 39. Hanlon's Razor
- **Source**: Hanlon
- **Definition**: Never attribute to malice what can be adequately explained by stupidity.
- **Application**: Conflict resolution, competitor analysis, team dynamics.

### 40. Sunk Cost Fallacy
- **Source**: Behavioral economics
- **Definition**: Past costs should not influence future decisions.
- **Application**: Project cancellation, pivot decisions, investment exit.

### 41. Hell Yeah or No
- **Source**: Sivers
- **Definition**: If it's not a clear enthusiastic yes, it's a no.
- **Application**: Opportunity filtering, time management, focus.

### 42. Pain + Reflection = Progress
- **Source**: Dalio
- **Definition**: Failures processed honestly become principles that prevent recurrence.
- **Application**: Post-mortems, personal development, organizational learning.

## Leadership & Organization

### 43. Golden Circle (WHY-HOW-WHAT)
- **Source**: Sinek
- **Definition**: Start communication with purpose (WHY), then method (HOW), then product (WHAT).
- **Application**: Brand messaging, leadership communication, culture building.

### 44. BRAVING Trust
- **Source**: Brown
- **Definition**: Trust = Boundaries + Reliability + Accountability + Vault + Integrity + Non-judgment + Generosity.
- **Application**: Team trust assessment, relationship repair.

### 45. Five Dysfunctions Pyramid
- **Source**: Lencioni
- **Definition**: Trust → Conflict → Commitment → Accountability → Results. Bottom-up resolution.
- **Application**: Team diagnosis, organizational health.

### 46. Servant Leadership
- **Source**: Greenleaf / Sinek
- **Definition**: Leaders serve those they lead. The cost of leadership is self-interest.
- **Application**: Management philosophy, culture design.

### 47. Idea Meritocracy
- **Source**: Dalio
- **Definition**: Best ideas win regardless of source. Believability-weighted decisions.
- **Application**: Meeting design, decision processes, culture.

## Entrepreneurship & Innovation

### 48. Productize Yourself
- **Source**: Naval
- **Definition**: Find specific knowledge + apply leverage + take accountability = wealth.
- **Application**: Career strategy, business design, personal brand.

### 49. Zero to One
- **Source**: Thiel
- **Definition**: Create something genuinely new vs copying what exists.
- **Application**: Innovation strategy, startup evaluation, competitive positioning.

### 50. Blitzscaling
- **Source**: Hoffman
- **Definition**: Prioritize speed over efficiency in winner-take-most markets.
- **Application**: Growth strategy, market entry, resource allocation.

### 51. Enough
- **Source**: Sivers / Chouinard
- **Definition**: Define what's sufficient and stop chasing more.
- **Application**: Life design, business strategy, resource management.

### 52. ABZ Planning
- **Source**: Hoffman
- **Definition**: Plan A (current), Plan B (pivot), Plan Z (lifeboat).
- **Application**: Career planning, startup strategy, risk management.

## How to Use This Catalog

1. **Before any major decision**, scan the catalog for relevant models
2. **Apply minimum 3 models** from different disciplines (Munger's rule)
3. **Always include Inversion** — what would guarantee failure?
4. **Check for biases** — which cognitive biases might be at play?
5. **Verify circle of competence** — are you qualified to apply these models here?
6. **Seek lollapalooza** — are multiple forces acting in the same direction?
