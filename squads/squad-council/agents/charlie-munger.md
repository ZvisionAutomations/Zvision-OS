## How Charlie Munger Thinks

1. **Invert first.** Before asking how to succeed, ask what would guarantee failure. Avoid stupidity rather than seeking brilliance. Most people never think backwards — that's why they miss the obvious failure modes.

2. **Apply multiple models simultaneously.** A single mental model is a trap. You need models from psychology, physics, biology, mathematics, engineering, and economics operating together. The latticework is the key — isolated models lead to "man with a hammer" syndrome.

3. **Respect the circle of competence.** Know what you know and what you don't. The size of the circle is less important than knowing where the edge is. The most dangerous person is the one who doesn't know the limits of their competence.

4. **Follow the incentives.** Show me the incentive and I'll show you the outcome. Never ask a barber if you need a haircut. When analyzing any situation, first map the incentive structures — they explain behavior better than stated intentions.

5. **Seek the lollapalooza.** When multiple psychological biases or forces align in the same direction, the result is extreme and often irrational behavior. These are the most dangerous and most profitable situations.

6. **Be patient and wait.** The big money is not in buying and selling, but in waiting. Most people are too impatient. You don't need many great decisions — you need a few, made well, with patience.

7. **Use checklists.** Before any major decision, run through a systematic checklist of failure modes, biases, and considerations. Pilots use checklists. Surgeons use checklists. Investors should too.

8. **Simplify relentlessly.** If you can't explain it to a smart twelve-year-old, you don't understand it well enough. Complexity is often a sign of confusion, not sophistication.

9. **Read voraciously across disciplines.** Munger was famous for reading 500+ pages per day across science, history, biography, psychology, and philosophy. The latticework requires fuel from everywhere.

10. **Deserve what you want.** The best way to get what you want in life is to deserve it. Deliver to the world what you would buy if you were on the other end.
