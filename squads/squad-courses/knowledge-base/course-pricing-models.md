# Course Pricing Models

How to price and structure your course offer for maximum value and revenue.

---

## Pricing Model Overview

| Model | Best For | Price Range | Recurring? |
|-------|----------|-------------|-----------|
| **One-time** | Single course, clear outcome | $97–$2,000 | No |
| **Subscription** | Library of content, ongoing updates | $29–$197/mo | Yes |
| **Cohort** | High-touch, live experience | $500–$5,000 | Per cohort |
| **Freemium** | Audience building, lead gen | Free → paid | Hybrid |
| **Tiered** | Multiple access levels | 3 price points | Mixed |

---

## One-Time Payment

**Structure:** Student pays once, gets lifetime (or defined) access.

**When to use:**
- Clearly scoped course with defined outcome
- Building a catalog
- Price range: $97 (entry) → $997 (premium)

**Pricing factors:**
- Transformation size: bigger outcome = higher price
- Course length: longer ≠ more valuable, but signals effort
- Format: video > written (perceived value)
- Bonus value: workbooks, templates, community access

---

## Subscription

**Structure:** Monthly or annual recurring fee for access to content library.

**When to use:**
- Multiple courses to offer
- Regular content updates
- Want predictable revenue

**Pricing anchors:**
- Monthly: $29–$97
- Annual: 20–30% discount (increases LTV, reduces churn)

**Churn risk:** High if student "finishes" content. Must add new content regularly or pair with community.

---

## Cohort-Based

**Structure:** Fixed enrollment window, live sessions, defined duration.

**When to use:**
- High-transformation programs
- Community is core to the product
- Instructor can commit to live time

**Pricing:**
- Entry-level cohort: $500–$1,500
- Premium cohort (with mentoring): $2,000–$5,000
- Enterprise/team: custom

**Revenue math:** 20 students at $1,500 = $30,000 per cohort. 4 cohorts/year = $120,000.

---

## Freemium

**Structure:** Core content free, premium access paid.

**Tier split:**
- Free: 1–2 modules, limited exercises
- Paid: Full course + community + bonuses

**When to use:**
- No existing audience (build trust first)
- Lead magnet strategy
- Platform with marketplace (Udemy model)

**Conversion rate:** Expect 2–5% of free users to upgrade if funnel is tight.

---

## Tiered Pricing (Good / Better / Best)

**Structure:** Same course with 3 access levels.

| Tier | Price | Includes |
|------|-------|---------|
| Core | $197 | Course access only |
| Premium | $397 | Course + workbook + templates |
| VIP | $797 | Premium + group coaching + certificate |

**Psychology:** Middle tier is the anchor. Most buyers choose it.
**Revenue:** Tiering increases average order value by 20–40%.

---

## Price Anchoring Strategies

| Strategy | How It Works | Example |
|----------|-------------|---------|
| **High-low anchor** | Show premium first, offer is "a steal" by comparison | $1,997 crossed out → $497 |
| **Comparison anchor** | "Less than a single coaching session" | Session at $300 vs course at $297 |
| **Daily cost frame** | Break down to cost per day | "$497 = $1.35/day for a year" |
| **ROI frame** | What will they earn/save? | "My students earn back this in their first client" |

---

## Payment Options

- **Full payment:** Standard, highest margin
- **2-pay or 3-pay:** Increases accessibility, total is 10–20% more
- **12-month installment:** For premium ($1,000+) courses

**Rule:** Always offer full pay discount. Never charge more than 20% extra for installments.

---

## When to Raise Prices

- [ ] Course consistently sells out or fills up
- [ ] Student testimonials and results are strong
- [ ] Waiting list exists
- [ ] You've added significant content or support
- [ ] Competitor benchmark shows you're underpriced

**Raise incrementally:** 20–30% per raise. Test on next launch. Keep existing students at old price.
