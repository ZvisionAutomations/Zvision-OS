# Video Production Guide

Standards for producing professional course video content.

---

## Camera Setup

| Setting | Recommendation | Why |
|---------|---------------|-----|
| Position | Eye level, centered | Natural eye contact |
| Distance | 1–1.5m from camera | Fills frame without distortion |
| Background | Plain wall, bookshelf, or branded backdrop | No visual noise |
| Framing | Head + shoulders visible, 20% headroom | Professional composition |
| Rule of thirds | Eyes on upper-third line | More engaging than centered |

**Camera options (ranked by quality/cost):**
1. Mirrorless/DSLR with video output (best)
2. Webcam 1080p (Sony INZONE, Logitech Brio)
3. Smartphone on tripod (acceptable for B-roll)

---

## Lighting

| Setup | Description | Cost |
|-------|-------------|------|
| Key light | Main light, 45° angle, front-facing | $50–$300 |
| Fill light | Soften shadows opposite key light | $30–$150 |
| Backlight/hair light | Separate subject from background | Optional |
| Natural light | Window light, face it — never back to it | Free |

**Rules:**
- Soft light > hard light (diffuse with softbox or sheer curtain)
- Eliminate mixed color temperatures (don't mix daylight + tungsten)
- Check for shadows under eyes and chin

## Audio

**Priority:** Audio quality matters more than video quality.

| Source | Quality | Setup |
|--------|---------|-------|
| USB condenser mic (Blue Yeti, Rode NT-USB) | Excellent | Desk mount, close to mouth |
| Lav mic (Rode Wireless GO) | Excellent | Clip to lapel |
| Headset mic | Good | Position at cheek, not mouth |
| Laptop built-in | Poor | Avoid if possible |

**Audio checklist:**
- [ ] Record 10 seconds of room tone (silence) at session start
- [ ] Mic within 30–50cm of mouth
- [ ] Room treated: rugs, curtains, soft surfaces reduce echo
- [ ] No HVAC, fans, or street noise audible
- [ ] Peak level at -12dB to -6dB (never clip at 0dB)

## Scripting Patterns

| Pattern | When to Use | Result |
|---------|-------------|--------|
| **Full script** | Complex technical content, first-time recorder | Precise, can sound robotic |
| **Outline + bullets** | Experienced presenter | Natural, requires more takes |
| **Teleprompter** | Full script but want eye contact | Natural delivery |
| **Conversational** | Demos, Q&A, walkthroughs | Authentic, unpredictable |

**Script structure per lesson:**
1. Hook (10–20 sec) — Why should I watch this?
2. Preview (10 sec) — What will I learn?
3. Content — Main delivery
4. Summary (15 sec) — What did I learn?
5. CTA (10 sec) — What's next / what to do now?

## Screen Recording

**Best tools:**
- Loom — quick, shareable, adds face cam
- OBS Studio — free, full control
- Camtasia — record + edit in one
- Screenflow (Mac) — polished output

**Settings:**
- Resolution: 1920×1080 minimum
- Frame rate: 30fps
- Cursor: highlight on click, slow down movements
- Browser: clean profile, no extensions visible, tab bar hidden if possible
- Font size: 16pt minimum in all apps shown

---

## Editing Tempo

| Principle | Rule |
|-----------|------|
| Cut silence | Remove all pauses >0.5 sec |
| Jump cuts | Acceptable for talking-head content — keep energy |
| B-roll | Insert every 60–90 sec on long talking-head segments |
| Lower thirds | Name/title on first appearance only |
| Captions | Always — improves accessibility and retention |
| Music | Background only, -20dB under voice |

**Editing software:** DaVinci Resolve (free), Descript (AI-first, fast), Premiere Pro, Final Cut

---

## Intro and Outro

**Intro (under 10 seconds):**
- Course name or logo
- Episode/lesson title
- No long jingles — students will skip them

**Outro (under 15 seconds):**
- "In the next lesson..."
- One CTA (exercise, download, next video)
- No bloated "like and subscribe" for course content

---

## Production Checklist (per recording session)

- [ ] Camera position and focus checked
- [ ] Lighting balanced, no shadows on face
- [ ] Audio test recorded and reviewed
- [ ] Background clean and branded
- [ ] Phone on silent, notifications off
- [ ] Script or outline reviewed
- [ ] Water bottle on desk (off camera)
- [ ] First take: always throw away (warm-up take)
