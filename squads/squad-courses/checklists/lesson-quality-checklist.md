# Lesson Quality Checklist

Use this checklist when reviewing individual lessons. A lesson that fails 3 or more items requires revision before publication.

---

## Objective and Focus

- [ ] Lesson has a clearly stated learning objective
- [ ] Objective uses a Bloom's action verb (not "understand" or "learn")
- [ ] Lesson teaches exactly one concept or skill (not two or more)
- [ ] Lesson title matches the actual content taught
- [ ] Content stays on-topic — no tangents or unrelated material

---

## Hook and Opening

- [ ] Lesson opens with a hook (question, problem, surprising fact, or story)
- [ ] Hook is relevant to the audience's actual situation
- [ ] "Why this matters" is communicated in the first 30–60 seconds (or first paragraph)
- [ ] Objective or outcome is stated early (not buried at the end)

---

## Content Structure

- [ ] Content is organized into scannable sections (headers for written, clear segments for video)
- [ ] Each section contains one point — no section is overloaded
- [ ] At least one concrete example or case study is included
- [ ] Abstract concepts are followed immediately by a concrete application
- [ ] Complex ideas are introduced before applying them
- [ ] Jargon is defined when introduced (not assumed)

---

## Practice and Application

- [ ] Lesson includes at least one exercise, prompt, or action step
- [ ] The exercise is actionable (student can do it in their real context)
- [ ] The exercise is specific (not "reflect on this" without a clear prompt)
- [ ] Exercise directly connects to the lesson objective

---

## Checkpoint and Assessment

- [ ] Lesson ends with a knowledge check (quiz question, self-eval, or checkpoint prompt)
- [ ] Checkpoint aligns with the lesson objective
- [ ] Student can self-assess whether they achieved the objective

---

## Duration and Pace

- [ ] Video lesson is 5–12 minutes (or written lesson is 800–2,500 words)
- [ ] Lesson doesn't feel rushed or padded
- [ ] Estimated duration matches actual delivery

---

## Accessibility and Language

- [ ] Language is appropriate for the target audience (not over or under their level)
- [ ] No unexplained acronyms or field-specific jargon
- [ ] Video captions are present (for video lessons)
- [ ] Text has sufficient contrast for readability (for written lessons)
- [ ] Images or diagrams have descriptive labels or alt text

---

## Takeaways and Closing

- [ ] Lesson ends with 3–5 key takeaways or a summary
- [ ] Closing includes a next step or bridge to the next lesson
- [ ] Student knows what to do with what they just learned
