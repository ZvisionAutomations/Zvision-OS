# Prospecting & Pipeline Generation

## Predictable Revenue Model (Aaron Ross)

### Three Pipeline Sources
```
SEEDS — Referrals & Word-of-Mouth
  Source: Existing clients, partners, network
  Conversion rate: 50-70% (highest)
  Cost: Very low
  Scalability: Limited (depends on base size)
  Strategy: Advocacy program, referral incentives, NPS follow-up

NETS — Inbound Marketing
  Source: Content, SEO, paid ads, events, social
  Conversion rate: 10-25%
  Cost: Medium (content + distribution)
  Scalability: High (compounding over time)
  Strategy: Content funnel, lead magnets, nurture sequences

SPEARS — Outbound Prospecting
  Source: SDR team, targeted outreach, ABM
  Conversion rate: 5-15%
  Cost: High (people + tools)
  Scalability: High (linear with headcount)
  Strategy: ICP targeting, multi-channel sequences, personalization
```

### Pipeline Source Mix (Healthy)
```
Recommended distribution:
  Seeds: 20-30% of pipeline (highest quality, hardest to scale)
  Nets: 30-40% of pipeline (scalable, compounding)
  Spears: 30-40% of pipeline (controllable, predictable)

Dangerous distributions:
  ⚠️ >60% from seeds → Over-reliant on referrals, not scalable
  ⚠️ >70% from one source → Single point of failure
  ⚠️ <10% from inbound → Not building long-term content asset
```

## Outbound Prospecting Framework

### Multi-Channel Sequence Design
```
PROSPECTING SEQUENCE — 21 DAYS

Day 1: LinkedIn — Connect request (personalized note)
  "Hi [Name], I noticed [specific observation about their company].
  Would love to connect."

Day 2: Email #1 — The Hook
  Subject: "[Observation] at [Company]"
  Body: Insight + question (no pitch)
  Goal: Earn the reply

Day 5: LinkedIn — Engage with their content
  Like, comment thoughtfully on recent post
  Goal: Build visibility

Day 7: Email #2 — Value Add
  Subject: "Re: [Original subject]"
  Body: Share relevant resource (case study, article, data)
  Goal: Demonstrate expertise

Day 10: Phone — First Call Attempt
  Voicemail script: 30 seconds max
  Reference the emails: "I sent a few notes about [topic]..."
  Goal: Create urgency to check email

Day 12: Email #3 — Social Proof
  Subject: "How [Similar Company] solved [problem]"
  Body: Brief case study relevant to their challenge
  Goal: Build credibility

Day 15: LinkedIn — Direct Message
  Reference previous touchpoints
  Ask a specific question: "Are you seeing [challenge] this quarter?"
  Goal: Start conversation

Day 18: Email #4 — The Breakup
  Subject: "Should I close your file?"
  Body: Acknowledge they're busy, offer one more value
  Goal: Create FOMO or get honest "not interested"

Day 21: Final Assessment
  Responded → Route to sales process
  No response → Move to long-term nurture (quarterly touch)
```

### Email Best Practices
```
SUBJECT LINE RULES:
  ✓ Short (4-7 words)
  ✓ Personalized (company name or observation)
  ✓ Curiosity-driven (question or surprising stat)
  ✗ No "Quick question" (overused, spam signal)
  ✗ No ALL CAPS or exclamation marks
  ✗ No "We" as first word (self-oriented)

BODY RULES:
  ✓ Under 100 words (mobile-friendly)
  ✓ One clear CTA (not multiple asks)
  ✓ About THEM, not about you
  ✓ Social proof embedded naturally
  ✗ No attachments on first email
  ✗ No "I hope this finds you well"
  ✗ No long company descriptions

CTA HIERARCHY (easiest first):
  1. "Worth a 15-min conversation?" (low commitment)
  2. "Would it be helpful if I sent [resource]?" (give before ask)
  3. "Can I share how we helped [similar company]?" (social proof)
  Avoid: "Are you free Tuesday at 2pm?" (too specific too early)
```

## ICP (Ideal Customer Profile)

### Three-Layer ICP Model
```
LAYER 1: FIRMOGRAPHIC (Company-level)
  Industry: [target industries]
  Revenue: R$[min] - R$[max]
  Employee count: [range]
  Geography: [markets served]
  Growth stage: [startup / scale-up / enterprise]
  Technology: [tech stack signals]

LAYER 2: STAKEHOLDER (Person-level)
  Title/Role: [target titles]
  Seniority: [C-level, VP, Director, Manager]
  Department: [Marketing, Sales, Growth, etc.]
  Responsibilities: [what they own]
  Pain points: [role-specific challenges]
  Success metrics: [what they're measured on]

LAYER 3: SITUATIONAL (Timing signals)
  Trigger events:
    □ New funding round
    □ New CMO/VP Marketing hired
    □ Rapid growth (headcount increasing)
    □ Competitor launched new campaign
    □ Annual planning cycle
    □ Current agency contract expiring
    □ Job postings for marketing roles (building team)
    □ Recent negative press or market shift
```

### ICP Scoring
| Criterion | Weight | Score (1-5) | Weighted |
|-----------|--------|-------------|---------|

| Tech stack alignment | 10% | | |

| **Total ICP Score** | 100% | | /5 |

### Pipeline Hygiene Checklist
```
WEEKLY PIPELINE REVIEW:
  □ All deals have next step with date
  □ No deals in same stage > [threshold] days
  □ All deals have MEDDIC score updated
  □ Forecast category accurate (commit/upside/long-shot)
  □ Stale deals addressed (advance, re-qualify, or remove)
  □ New pipeline added (net pipeline positive)

MONTHLY DEEP CLEAN:
  □ Win/loss analysis on closed deals
  □ Stage conversion rates calculated
  □ Average cycle time by segment
  □ Pipeline source attribution reviewed
  □ Forecast accuracy vs actual analyzed
```

## Cold Outreach Personalization Framework

### Levels of Personalization
```
LEVEL 1: SPRAY & PRAY (Avoid)
  "Hi, we help companies with marketing..."
  Personalization: None
  Response rate: <1%

LEVEL 2: SEGMENT-BASED
  "Hi [Name], as a [Title] at a [Industry] company..."
  Personalization: Title + Industry
  Response rate: 2-5%

LEVEL 3: ACCOUNT-BASED
  "Hi [Name], I noticed [Company] recently [specific event]..."
  Personalization: Company-specific observation
  Response rate: 5-12%

LEVEL 4: INSIGHT-BASED (Target)
  "Hi [Name], I was looking at [Company]'s [specific asset]
  and noticed [specific insight]. Companies in [their space]
  typically see [X] when they [approach]. Would it be worth
  exploring if that applies to your situation?"
  Personalization: Specific insight + relevance
  Response rate: 12-25%
```
