# Sales Qualification Methodology

## MEDDIC / MEDDPICC

### Framework Completo
| Letter | Element | Key Question | Evidence Required |
|--------|---------|-------------|-------------------|
| **M** | Metrics | What measurable outcome justifies this investment? | Specific KPI improvement target |
| **E** | Economic Buyer | Who has budget authority and final sign-off? | Name, title, meeting scheduled |
| **D** | Decision Criteria | What factors will determine the choice? | Written or verbal criteria list |
| **D** | Decision Process | What steps, approvals, and timeline to decision? | Process map with dates |
| **P** | Paper Process | What's the procurement/legal process? | Legal review timeline, MSA requirements |
| **I** | Identify Pain | Is the pain compelling enough to drive action? | Client-articulated pain with business impact |
| **C** | Champion | Who internally advocates for our solution? | Named contact with demonstrated advocacy |
| **C** | Competition | Who else is being evaluated? | Named competitors or alternatives |

### Scoring Model
```
Each element: 0 (Unknown), 1 (Identified), 2 (Validated)
Maximum score: 16/16 (MEDDPICC) or 12/12 (MEDDIC)

Qualification thresholds:
  12-16: STRONG — invest fully, prioritize
  8-11:  MEDIUM — fill gaps before heavy investment
  4-7:   WEAK — re-qualify or nurture
  0-3:   UNQUALIFIED — do not pursue actively
```

### When to Use
- **Always** for deals > R$50K ARR
- **Recommended** for all deals > R$10K ARR
- Decision stage progression gates in CRM

## BANT

### Framework
| Element | Question | Threshold |
|---------|---------|-----------|
| **B**udget | Is there allocated budget? | Budget exists or can be created |
| **A**uthority | Are we talking to the decision maker? | Access to economic buyer confirmed |
| **N**eed | Is there a genuine business need? | Pain identified and acknowledged |
| **T**imeline | Is there urgency to act? | Decision within 6 months |

### Limitations
- Too binary (yes/no) — lacks nuance
- Budget-first bias may disqualify good opportunities early
- Best used as initial screening, not full qualification
- Supplement with MEDDIC for complex deals

## SPIN Selling (Neil Rackham)

### Question Types
```
S — SITUATION QUESTIONS
  Purpose: Understand current context
  Example: "How is your marketing team structured today?"
  Risk: Too many → feels like interrogation
  Rule: Max 3-4 per call, do pre-research to minimize

P — PROBLEM QUESTIONS
  Purpose: Identify explicit difficulties
  Example: "What's your biggest challenge with lead generation?"
  Impact: Identifies stated needs
  Rule: Go deeper than surface — ask "why" and "how long"

I — IMPLICATION QUESTIONS
  Purpose: Develop problem severity and urgency
  Example: "If this continues, what happens to your Q4 targets?"
  Impact: Creates urgency, quantifies cost of inaction
  Rule: THE MOST IMPORTANT — this is where deals are won

N — NEED-PAYOFF QUESTIONS
  Purpose: Get prospect to articulate value of solution
  Example: "If you could solve X, what would that mean for revenue?"
  Impact: Prospect sells themselves on the value
  Rule: Let THEM describe the value, don't tell them
```

### SPIN in Agency Context
| Question Type | Agency-Specific Examples |
|--------------|------------------------|
| Situation | "Walk me through a typical campaign launch process" |
| Problem | "Where do you lose the most time between idea and execution?" |
| Implication | "When campaigns launch late, how does that affect your quarterly pipeline?" |
| Need-Payoff | "If you could cut campaign launch time in half, what would that free up?" |

## Challenger Sale (Dixon & Adamson)

### Three Competencies
1. **Teach** — Bring new insights the client hasn't considered
2. **Tailor** — Customize the message to each stakeholder
3. **Take Control** — Confidently guide the buying process

### Commercial Teaching Sequence
```
1. THE WARMER
   "Based on our research, companies like yours typically face..."
   → Show you understand their world

2. THE REFRAME
   "What most people don't realize is..."
   → Challenge a commonly held assumption

3. RATIONAL DROWNING
   "The data shows this costs companies like yours R$X/year..."
   → Overwhelm with evidence of the problem

4. EMOTIONAL IMPACT
   "One VP we worked with said: 'I was spending 40% of my time
   on firefighting instead of strategy'..."
   → Make it personal and relatable

5. A NEW WAY
   "There's a different approach..."
   → Present the alternative (NOT your product yet)

6. YOUR SOLUTION
   "This is specifically how we help..."
   → ONLY NOW connect to your capabilities
```

### When to Use
- Commoditized markets where differentiation is hard
- Sophisticated buyers who "know everything"
- Complex sales with multiple stakeholders
- Situations where status quo is the real competitor

## Sandler Selling System

### 7 Steps
1. **Bonding & Rapport** — Establish trust and equal footing
2. **Up-Front Contract** — Agree on agenda, outcomes, next steps BEFORE the conversation
3. **Pain** — Uncover emotional and business pain (surface → business → personal)
4. **Budget** — Qualify budget willingness (not just availability)
5. **Decision** — Map the complete decision process
6. **Fulfillment** — Present solution ONLY after pain, budget, decision confirmed
7. **Post-Sell** — Inoculate against buyer's remorse

### Key Sandler Principles
```
"No mutual mystification"
  → Be transparent about process, pricing, and fit

"Don't spill your candy in the lobby"
  → Don't give away your solution before qualifying

"People buy emotionally, then justify logically"
  → Find the personal pain behind the business pain

"Reversing" technique
  → Answer questions with questions to understand real concern
  Client: "How much does it cost?"
  Sandler: "Great question. Before I answer — what were you expecting?"

"Negative reverse selling"
  → Gently suggest the solution might NOT be for them
  "I'm not sure we're the right fit — can you tell me more about X?"
  → Reduces defensiveness, increases engagement
```

## Gap Selling (Keenan)

### Core Model
```
CURRENT STATE → GAP → FUTURE STATE

Current State:
  Technical environment: What tools, processes, team
  Business results: Current metrics, performance
  Impact: How current state affects business

Future State:
  Desired technical environment
  Desired business results
  Expected impact

The GAP:
  The difference between current and future state
  = The TRUE SIZE OF THE OPPORTUNITY
  = The JUSTIFICATION FOR INVESTMENT
```

### Gap Calculation
```
Gap Value = (Future State Value - Current State Value) × Time

Example:
  Current conversion rate: 2% → R$1M/year revenue
  Future conversion rate: 4% → R$2M/year revenue
  Gap value: R$1M/year additional revenue
  Investment: R$200K/year agency fees
  ROI: 5x annual return
```

## Framework Selection Guide

| Scenario | Primary | Supplementary |
|----------|---------|---------------|
| Complex enterprise deal (>R$100K) | MEDDPICC | Challenger (teaching) |
| Mid-market consultative sale | SPIN | Gap Selling (quantification) |
| Competitive displacement | Challenger | MEDDIC (qualification) |
| Transactional / quick sale | BANT | Sandler (up-front contract) |
| Expansion / upsell (existing client) | Gap Selling | SPIN (new needs) |
| Commoditized market | Challenger | Sandler (differentiation via process) |
