# Agency Product Management Patterns

## Purpose
Padroes especificos de product management em contexto de agencia digital, onde a dinamica e fundamentalmente diferente de produto interno.

## Agency vs In-House PM

| Dimension | In-House PM | Agency PM |
|-----------|-----------|-----------|
| Authority | Full decision power | Advisory + influence |
| Knowledge | Deep domain expertise | Broad multi-industry |
| Timeline | Long-term (years) | Engagement-based (months) |
| Clients | Internal stakeholders | External paying clients |
| Team | Dedicated, stable | Shared, rotating |
| Metrics | Own the numbers | Influence the numbers |
| Budget | Own budget | Client budget |
| Success | Product metrics | Client satisfaction + metrics |

## Core Agency PM Patterns

### Pattern 1: Dual Roadmap System
**Problem:** Clients want dates and certainty. Product needs flexibility.
**Solution:** Maintain two roadmap views.
- Internal: Sprint-level, features, dependencies, tech debt
- Client: Outcomes, Now/Next/Later, confidence levels
**Rule:** Never share internal roadmap directly with client.

### Pattern 2: Rich Mironov Reframe
**Problem:** Clients ask for features, not problems.
**Solution:** Always reframe before accepting.
- Client says: "We need a dashboard"
- PM asks: "What decisions would you make with that data?"
- Real need: "I need to know if our campaign is working"
**Rule:** 5 Reframing Questions before any commitment.

### Pattern 3: Scope Negotiation (Not Scope Creep)
**Problem:** Scope changes are inevitable. Unmanaged = scope creep.
**Solution:** Formalized change process.
- Every addition trades with a removal or extends timeline
- Always present 3+ options (never just "yes" or "no")
- Document every change decision
**Rule:** No verbal scope changes are binding.

### Pattern 4: Quick Win Strategy
**Problem:** New engagements need trust fast.
**Solution:** Deliver visible value within 30 days.
- Small but impactful improvement
- Demonstrates competence and pace
- Builds trust capital for longer-term work
**Rule:** Quick win before strategic work.

### Pattern 5: Knowledge Transfer as Insurance
**Problem:** Agency engagement will end. Knowledge must survive.
**Solution:** Continuous documentation, not end-of-project handoff.
- Decision log updated in real-time
- Knowledge base maintained throughout
- 10-document handoff package always ready
**Rule:** If you got hit by a bus, could someone else continue?

### Pattern 6: Multi-Client Capacity Management
**Problem:** Shared teams serve multiple clients.
**Solution:** Explicit allocation with focus factor adjustment.
```
Focus Factor:
  1 client = 0.80 productivity
  2 clients = 0.65 productivity
  3+ clients = 0.50 productivity
Context-switching penalty: -15% per additional client
```
**Rule:** Never allocate >3 clients per PM.

### Pattern 7: Client Champion Identification
**Problem:** Losing your champion = losing the engagement.
**Solution:** Always identify and nurture multiple champions.
- Primary champion: Day-to-day decision maker
- Executive sponsor: Budget authority
- Technical champion: Engineering alignment
**Rule:** Never have single point of failure.

### Pattern 8: Value-Based Reporting
**Problem:** Clients see cost, not value.
**Solution:** Always frame work in value terms.
- "We fixed 12 bugs" → "Users can now complete checkout 40% faster"
- "We spent 80 hours" → "We delivered 3 capabilities worth $X in efficiency"
**Rule:** Lead with outcomes, not outputs.

## Anti-Patterns to Avoid

| Anti-Pattern | Why It Fails | Better Approach |
|-------------|-------------|-----------------|
| Order-taking | Client loses respect, PM adds no value | Partner mode: challenge and advise |
| Over-promising | Sets unrealistic expectations | Under-promise, over-deliver |
| Hiding problems | Trust destroyed when discovered | Transparent early, bring solutions |
| Ignoring tech debt | Velocity decreases, bugs increase | Protect 15% budget always |
| Single-threaded | Key person risk | Cross-train, document, share |
| Feature factory | No strategy, just output | Connect every feature to outcome |
| Vanity metrics | Looks good, means nothing | Focus on actionable metrics only |

## Agency PM Career Development
| Level | Scope | Key Skills |
|-------|-------|-----------|
| Junior PM | Task execution, single client | Process following, communication |
| PM | Feature ownership, 1-2 clients | Prioritization, stakeholder management |
| Senior PM | Product ownership, 2-3 clients | Strategy, client relationship, mentoring |
| PM Lead | Multi-product, portfolio | Portfolio management, team building |
| Head of Product | Practice leadership | Methodology, business development |
