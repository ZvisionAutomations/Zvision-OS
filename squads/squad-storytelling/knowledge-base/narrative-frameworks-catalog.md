## 1. The Hero's Journey (Monomyth)

**Creator:** Joseph Campbell
**Source:** The Hero with a Thousand Faces (1949)
**Agent:** joseph-campbell

### 12-Stage Simplified Version
1. Ordinary World
2. Call to Adventure
3. Refusal of the Call
4. Meeting the Mentor
5. Crossing the Threshold
6. Tests, Allies, and Enemies
7. Approach to the Inmost Cave
8. The Ordeal
9. Reward (Seizing the Sword)
10. The Road Back
11. Resurrection
12. Return with the Elixir

### 8 Archetypes
Hero, Mentor, Threshold Guardian, Herald, Shapeshifter, Shadow, Trickster, Ally

### When to Use
- Epic transformations with mythic resonance
- Stories that need to feel universal and timeless
- Brand origin stories that feel legendary
- Any narrative where the protagonist undergoes profound change

## 2. The Story Circle (8 Steps)

**Creator:** Dan Harmon
**Source:** Channel 101 / Community writers' room
**Agent:** dan-harmon

### Structure
1. **YOU** — Zone of comfort (status quo)
2. **NEED** — Something is wanted
3. **GO** — Enter unfamiliar situation
4. **SEARCH** — Adapt and struggle
5. **FIND** — Get what was wanted
6. **TAKE** — Pay a heavy price
7. **RETURN** — Go back to familiar
8. **CHANGE** — Having been transformed

### Key Concept: Fractal Narrative
The circle applies at every level: series, season, episode, act, scene.

### When to Use
- TV episodes and series
- Any episodic or serialized content
- When story structure needs to be simple and practical
- Character-driven narratives where transformation is the point

## 3. Save the Cat Beat Sheet (15 Beats)

**Creator:** Blake Snyder
**Source:** Save the Cat! (2005)
**Agent:** blake-snyder

### Beat Summary
| Beat | Page | Purpose |
|------|------|---------|
| Opening Image | 1 | Before snapshot |
| Theme Stated | 5 | Lesson planted |
| Set-Up | 1-10 | World established |
| Catalyst | 12 | Inciting incident |
| Debate | 12-25 | Should I go? |
| Break into Two | 25 | Hero's choice |
| B Story | 30 | Theme carrier |
| Fun and Games | 30-55 | Promise of premise |
| Midpoint | 55 | False victory/defeat |
| Bad Guys Close In | 55-75 | Pressure mounts |
| All Is Lost | 75 | Whiff of death |
| Dark Night of Soul | 75-85 | Despair |
| Break into Three | 85 | A+B cross |
| Finale | 85-110 | Transformation |
| Final Image | 110 | After snapshot |

### 10 Genre Types
Monster in the House, Golden Fleece, Out of the Bottle, Dude with a Problem, Rites of Passage, Buddy Love, Whydunit, Fool Triumphant, Institutionalized, Superhero

### When to Use
- Commercial screenplays and novels
- Any narrative needing tight, audience-tested structure
- When crafting loglines
- Genre classification and convention adherence

## 4. STRONG Method (6 Phases)

**Creator:** Oren Klaff
**Source:** Pitch Anything (2011)
**Agent:** oren-klaff

### Phases
1. **S**et the Frame — Establish dominance
2. **T**ell the Story — Narrative over data
3. **R**eveal the Intrigue — Unexpected tension
4. **O**ffer the Prize — You are the prize
5. **N**ail the Hookpoint — Buyer leans in
6. **G**et the Deal — Close with clarity

### 5 Frames
Power, Time, Analyst, Prize (offensive), Intrigue (recovery)

### When to Use
- Investor pitches and fundraising
- High-stakes sales meetings
- Any situation requiring frame control
- Negotiations where status dynamics matter

## 5. Sparkline

**Creator:** Nancy Duarte
**Source:** Resonate (2010)
**Agent:** nancy-duarte

### Structure
- Oscillate between **What Is** (current reality) and **What Could Be** (envisioned future)
- Include **S.T.A.R. Moment** (Something They'll Always Remember)
- End with **New Bliss** (transformed world vision)
- **Call to Action** (specific next step)

### When to Use
- Keynote presentations and TED-style talks
- Data storytelling and business presentations
- Any communication needing audience transformation
- When the audience must be moved to specific action

## 6. 4 Stories Framework

**Creator:** Kindra Hall
**Source:** Stories That Stick (2019)
**Agent:** kindra-hall

### The 4 Stories
1. **Value Story** — Communicates product/service value (for sales)
2. **Founder Story** — Creates trust through origin (for brand)
3. **Purpose Story** — Galvanizes teams (for culture)
4. **Customer Story** — Social proof through narrative (for marketing)

### Structure: Normal → Explosion → New Normal

### When to Use
- Sales conversations and marketing campaigns
- Brand building and employer branding
- When identifying Story Gaps in business communication
- Personal narrative development (self-stories)

## 7. ABT (And-But-Therefore)

**Creator:** Randy Olson / Park Howell
**Source:** Houston, We Have a Narrative (2015)
**Agent:** park-howell

### Structure
- **And** — Context, status quo, agreement
- **But** — Conflict, tension, the problem
- **Therefore** — Resolution, action, consequence

### Anti-Pattern: AAA (And-And-And) — boring, no tension

### When to Use
- Elevator pitches and executive summaries
- Email subject lines and social media
- Converting any AAA communication to narrative
- Quick narrative fitness check for any message

## 8. Story Cycle System (10 Steps)

**Creator:** Park Howell
**Source:** Brand Bewitchery
**Agent:** park-howell

### Steps
1. Backstory → 2. Desire → 3. Resistance → 4. Adventure → 5. Allies & Enemies → 6. Trial → 7. Death → 8. Rebirth → 9. Return → 10. Encore

### Key Principle
Customer is the hero. Brand is the guide/mentor.

### When to Use
- Complete brand storytelling strategy
- Content marketing narrative frameworks
- Customer journey mapping through story lens

## 9. Status Transactions

**Creator:** Keith Johnstone
**Source:** Impro (1979)
**Agent:** keith-johnstone

### Core Concept
Every interaction involves a status transaction (high or low). Status is behavioral, not social rank.

### Key Techniques
- Yes, And (accept and build)
- Offers and Blocks
- Tilting, Advancing, Reincorporation
- Platform-Tilt-Resolution

### When to Use
- Creative unblocking and overcoming fear
- Understanding interpersonal dynamics
- Improvisation training and workshops
- When spontaneity and play are needed

---

## 10. Public Narrative (Self/Us/Now)

**Creator:** Marshall Ganz
**Source:** Harvard Kennedy School / Community Organizing
**Agent:** marshall-ganz

### Three Stories
1. **Story of Self** — Why I care (personal values)
2. **Story of Us** — Why we care (collective identity)
3. **Story of Now** — What we must do (urgent action)

### When to Use
- Social movements and activism
- Organizational change initiatives
- Leadership communication requiring moral authority
- Any narrative that must translate values into collective action
