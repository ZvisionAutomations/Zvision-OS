# Brand Strategy Frameworks Catalog

Scholarly frameworks from elite brand strategists, with sourcing and application guidance.

## David Aaker — Brand Equity Model (5 Dimensions)
**Source:** "Managing Brand Equity" (1991), "Building Strong Brands" (1996)
- **Brand Loyalty:** Depth of commitment. Reduces marketing costs, barrier to competitors.
- **Brand Awareness:** Recognition → recall → top-of-mind. Anchor for associations.
- **Perceived Quality:** Consumer assessment vs alternatives. Enables premium pricing.
- **Brand Associations:** Mental connections — attributes, benefits, personality, user imagery.
- **Proprietary Assets:** Patents, trademarks, IP, channel relationships.

## David Aaker — Brand Identity Model (4 Perspectives)
- Brand as Product (scope, attributes, quality, uses, users, origin)
- Brand as Organization (attributes, local vs global)
- Brand as Person (personality traits, brand-customer relationships)
- Brand as Symbol (visual imagery, brand heritage)
Core Elements (2-5, timeless) + Extended Elements (3-5, evolving) + Brand Essence (single theme)

## David Aaker — Brand Architecture Spectrum
- Branded House (Google, Virgin, BMW)
- Sub-Brands (Apple iPhone, Microsoft Office)
- Endorsed Brands (Marriott's Courtyard)
- House of Brands (P&G: Tide, Pampers, Gillette)

## David Aaker — Brand Relevance Framework
Real growth = create new categories making competitors irrelevant.
- Brand Preference: "my brand is better" — incremental
- Brand Relevance: new category with "must haves" — competitors become irrelevant

## Al Ries — Positioning (22 Immutable Laws)
**Source:** "Positioning" (1981), "The 22 Immutable Laws of Marketing" (1993)
Key laws: Law of Leadership, Law of Category, Law of Mind, Law of Focus, Law of Exclusivity, Law of Ladder, Law of Duality, Law of Opposite.

## Jean-Noel Kapferer — Brand Identity Prism (6 Facets)
**Source:** "The New Strategic Brand Management" (1992)
- Physique (tangible features), Personality (character), Culture (values), Relationship (bond with consumer), Reflection (consumer image), Self-Image (internal mirror)

## Kapferer — Luxury Anti-Laws
24 anti-laws of luxury marketing — e.g., don't pander to wishes, don't respond to demand, dominate the client, make it difficult to buy.

## Marty Neumeier — The Brand Gap & Zag
**Source:** "The Brand Gap" (2003), "Zag" (2006)
- Brand = gut feeling about a product/service/company
- Zag: radical differentiation through "onliness" statement
- Brand Gap: bridge between strategy and creativity

## Byron Sharp — How Brands Grow
**Source:** "How Brands Grow" (2010)
- Mental availability: be thought of in buying situations
- Physical availability: be easy to find and buy
- Distinctive assets > differentiation
- Reach > frequency
- Double Jeopardy Law: small brands suffer twice

## Donald Miller — StoryBrand (SB7)
**Source:** "Building a StoryBrand" (2017)
1. A Character (customer) 2. Has a Problem (villain) 3. Meets a Guide (you) 4. Who Gives Them a Plan 5. Calls Them to Action 6. Helps Them Avoid Failure 7. Ends in Success
