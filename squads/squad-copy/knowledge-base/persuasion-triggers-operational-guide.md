# Persuasion Triggers — Operational Guide

Comprehensive guide for the 7 Cialdini principles + additional behavioral triggers.

## The 7 Cialdini Principles

### 1. Reciprocity
- **Definition:** People feel obligated to return favors
- **Patterns:** Free value first (guides, audits, templates), unexpected gifts (bonus content), personalized recommendations
- **Anti-patterns:** Strings attached, guilt-tripping, excessive follow-up after giving
- **Measurement:** Response rate lift, engagement after free value delivery
- **A/B Test Ideas:** Free audit vs. paid consultation CTA, with vs. without bonus content

### 2. Commitment & Consistency
- **Definition:** People align future actions with past commitments
- **Patterns:** Micro-commitments (quiz, calculator, small yes), foot-in-door (free trial → paid), public commitments (testimonials)
- **Anti-patterns:** Bait-and-switch, escalating commitments deceptively
- **Measurement:** Completion rates, trial-to-paid conversion, step-through rates
- **A/B Test Ideas:** Multi-step form vs. single form, quiz funnel vs. direct CTA

### 3. Social Proof
- **Definition:** People follow the actions of others
- **Patterns:** Testimonials with specifics, client logos, case studies with numbers, "X customers served", real-time activity ("5 people viewing")
- **Anti-patterns:** Fake reviews, manufactured testimonials, inflated numbers
- **Measurement:** Conversion lift with vs. without social proof elements
- **A/B Test Ideas:** Testimonial placement, video vs. text testimonials, specific numbers vs. vague claims

### 4. Authority
- **Definition:** People defer to credible experts
- **Patterns:** Credentials and certifications, expert endorsements, data-backed claims, industry awards, media mentions
- **Anti-patterns:** False authority, irrelevant credentials, appeal to unrelated expertise
- **Measurement:** Trust metrics, time on page, bounce rate reduction
- **A/B Test Ideas:** With vs. without credentials, expert quote vs. no quote

### 5. Liking
- **Definition:** People say yes to those they like
- **Patterns:** Similarity (shared values, language), genuine compliments, association with positive outcomes, authentic storytelling
- **Anti-patterns:** Excessive flattery, fake relatability, manufactured persona
- **Measurement:** Engagement rates, social shares, reply rates
- **A/B Test Ideas:** Personal story vs. corporate tone, founder voice vs. brand voice

### 6. Scarcity
- **Definition:** People value what's limited
- **Patterns:** Limited time (deadline-driven offers), limited quantity (cohort-based), exclusive access (invite-only), seasonal availability
- **Anti-patterns:** Fake scarcity (always "ending soon"), manufactured urgency, countdown timers that reset
- **Measurement:** Urgency-driven conversion rate, time-to-decision
- **A/B Test Ideas:** With vs. without countdown, specific deadline vs. vague urgency

### 7. Unity
- **Definition:** People favor those in their in-group
- **Patterns:** Shared identity ("for agency owners"), tribe language, us-vs-them framing (without being negative), community belonging
- **Anti-patterns:** Exclusionary language, divisive messaging, false belonging
- **Measurement:** Community engagement, brand affinity scores, referral rates
- **A/B Test Ideas:** Generic vs. identity-specific messaging, community CTA vs. individual CTA

## Additional Behavioral Triggers

### Loss Aversion
People feel losses ~2x more than equivalent gains. Frame outcomes as what they'll lose by NOT acting, not just what they'll gain.

### Anchoring
First number sets the reference point. Show premium price first, then the actual offer. Show "was $X, now $Y" with genuine original price.

### Framing Effect
Same information, different frame = different decision. "95% success rate" > "5% failure rate". Frame positively for benefits, negatively for urgency.

### Endowment Effect
People overvalue what they already possess. Free trials, samples, and "it's already yours" language leverage this.

## Ethical Boundaries
- Never mislead about product capabilities or results
- Never manufacture false urgency or scarcity
- Respect customer autonomy — inform, don't manipulate
- All claims must be substantiated
- Comply with advertising regulations (CONAR, FTC, ASA)
- When in doubt, ask: "Would I be comfortable if this tactic were publicly disclosed?"
