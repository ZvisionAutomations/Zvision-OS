# Legendary Copywriters — Frameworks Reference

Elite copywriting frameworks from the greatest direct response copywriters, with sourcing and application.

## Gary Halbert — "The Prince of Print"
**Source:** "The Boron Letters," The Gary Halbert Letter

### Starving Crowd Principle
"If I get ONE advantage, I'd choose a starving crowd." Market selection > copy quality. Find desperate buyers BEFORE you write.

### A-Pile / B-Pile
Mail sorted into A-pile (personal, will read) and B-pile (junk). Your job: get into the A-pile. Technique: hand-addressed, first-class stamps, no teaser copy, feels like a letter from a friend.

### Star-Story-Solution (SSS)
1. **Star** — Introduce compelling character (often the reader)
2. **Story** — Tell dramatic, emotional story about their problem
3. **Solution** — Present product as natural, inevitable solution

### The Halbert Index (Swipe File)
Collect winning ads obsessively. Study what WORKED. Rewrite successful ads BY HAND to internalize patterns.

### Reason Why
Always give compelling reason WHY — why this offer, why this price, why act now. Without reason-why, prospects are suspicious.

## Eugene Schwartz — "The Einstein of Copywriting"
**Source:** "Breakthrough Advertising" (1966)

### 5 Levels of Market Awareness
| Level | Awareness | Headline Strategy |
|-------|-----------|-------------------|
| 1 | Most Aware | Name product, make offer |
| 2 | Product Aware | Show superiority |
| 3 | Solution Aware | Connect solution to product |
| 4 | Problem Aware | Agitate problem, introduce solution |
| 5 | Unaware | Lead with story/emotion |

### 5 Stages of Market Sophistication
1. Be first — simple direct claim
2. Enlarge the claim — bigger, better
3. Mechanism — explain HOW it works
4. Enlarged mechanism — improved mechanism
5. Identification — connect to prospect's identity

### Mass Desire Channeling
"Copy cannot create desire. It can only take existing desires and channel them toward your product."

## David Ogilvy — "The Father of Advertising"
**Source:** "Ogilvy on Advertising" (1983), "Confessions of an Advertising Man" (1963)

### Ogilvy's Rules
- "On average, 5x as many people read the headline as the body copy"
- "The consumer is not a moron. She is your wife."
- "Tell the truth, but make it fascinating"
- Research first, create second — "advertising people who ignore research are as dangerous as generals who ignore enemy signals"

### Big Idea Test
"It takes a big idea to attract the attention of consumers. Unless your advertising is built on a big idea, it will pass like a ship in the night."

## Dan Kennedy — "The Millionaire Maker"
**Source:** "The Ultimate Sales Letter," "No B.S." series

### Magnetic Marketing
Message-Market-Media match. The right message to the right market through the right media.

### Sales Letter Structure
1. Headline (most important element)
2. Opening (continue the headline's promise)
3. Build the case (logic + emotion)
4. Make the offer
5. Call to action (clear, specific, urgent)

## Russell Brunson
**Source:** "DotCom Secrets," "Expert Secrets," "Traffic Secrets"

### Hook-Story-Offer
Every piece of marketing: Hook (grab attention) → Story (build connection/desire) → Offer (present solution)

### Attractive Character Framework
4 types: Leader, Adventurer, Reporter, Reluctant Hero. Build identity around one.

### Value Ladder
Free → Front-end ($) → Middle ($$$) → Back-end ($$$$) → Continuity (recurring)

## Stefan Georgi — RMBC Method
**Source:** "The RMBC Method"
- **R** = Research (market, product, prospect)
- **M** = Mechanism (unique mechanism that makes it work)
- **B** = Brief (outline before writing)
- **C** = Copy (write using the brief)

## Claude Hopkins
**Source:** "Scientific Advertising" (1923)
- Test everything, track results
- "The time to advertise is when the other fellow isn't"
- Pre-emptive claim: first to claim a common benefit OWNS it
- Reason-why advertising: justify every claim
